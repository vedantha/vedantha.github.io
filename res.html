Tablet app for restaurants

  • Date: Aug - Oct 2015
  • Team Size: Three
  • Worked with: Neha Singhal, Eric Swartz

The Problem

Over the course of six weeks, we explored some common problems of restaurant goers. There are The most common one was wait time at nearly all stages of the process of walking in to a restaurant, and billing. We came up with a solution can help reduce waiting times : an application running on a tablet device placed under the surface of the table.


Solution process


Research

The research process involved interviews and field observations at restaurants.

Demographics and important data from the interviews:

  • Number of interviewees: 6
  • Age range: 19 to 27
  • 6/6 said they prefer to eat out with someone rather than alone
  • The preferred average waiting time for food was 15 minutes

Obervation notes:

We went to restaurants and observed the interactions, customers reactions, and other relevant details that could be of interest.

Key points:

The following are some of the most common problems that customers faced, based on the interviews and observations

  • Waiting time is the most common customer concern
  • Some customers find it difficult to choose, a menu should be able to help with that by giving more information about the dishes
  • Ease of calling the waiter
  • Quality and taste of food
