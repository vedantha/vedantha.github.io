The Problem

Over the course of six weeks, we explored some common problems of restaurant goers. The most common one was wait time at nearly all stages of the process: walking in to a restaurant, waiting for the waiter to attend the table, waiting to get food, and billing. We came up with a solution can help reduce waiting times, and improve the quality of service: an application running on a tablet device placed under the surface of the table.

Design process


Research

The research process involved interviews and field observations at restaurants.

Demographics and important data from the interviews:

  • Number of interviewees: 6
  • Age range: 19 to 27
  • 6/6 said they prefer to eat out with someone rather than alone
  • The preferred average waiting time for food was 15 minutes

Key points:

The following are some of the most common problems that customers faced, based on the interviews and observations

  • Waiting time is the most common customer concern
  • Some customers find it difficult to choose, a menu should be able to help with that by giving more information about the dishes
  • Ease of calling the waiter
  • Quality and taste of food
